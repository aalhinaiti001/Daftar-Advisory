From the founder

Why this practice exists.

Ahmad Al Hinaiti · Amman, 2026

Most mid-size businesses in KSA and the UAE cannot justify Big Four fees for the technical work they actually need — and they do not get senior attention from a Big Four firm even when they pay it. A partner takes the scoping call. A junior analyst does the work. The partner reviews a draft on the day of delivery. You speak to the partner; the work belongs to someone else.

Daftar Advisory is built around one answer to that problem: one practitioner does the work and stands behind every number. No pyramid. No handoff. The person on the scoping call is the same person who opens the Excel file, reads the contracts, writes the memo, and delivers the file.

The Big Four sell you an audit file.
Daftar sells the judgement that produced it.

I spent eight years inside PwC and EY before building this practice — PwC Amman, PwC Dubai, and EY Riyadh, across audit, IFRS advisory, and transaction support. That background gave me two things worth more than any credential: a working understanding of the file quality that sophisticated auditors actually expect, and a clear view of the gap between what a Big Four engagement promises and what it delivers to a mid-size client.

Every Daftar engagement is priced at a fixed fee, scoped to a written outcome, and delivered on a defined timeline — all named in the engagement letter before you commit. What the work covers and what it does not cover are both written down. The fee does not move unless the scope changes, and if the scope changes because I missed something at kick-off, I absorb the additional work. There are no surprises in the invoice.

The deliverable always ships with the working file: formulas intact, assumptions documented, sources cited. No proprietary models. No lock-in. What you receive at the end of an engagement is genuinely yours — to extend, audit, and hand to the next person without needing me in the room to explain it.

This practice does not take attest work. It does not sign audit opinions, file tax returns on behalf of clients, or provide CMA-licensed advisory. Where an engagement requires a licensed signature, I refer the work to a firm better placed to do it, and I usually provide a name. The boundaries are not a limitation — they are the honesty of knowing where competent advisory ends.

If there is a fit, a written scope and fee will be in your inbox within 48 hours of the scoping call. If there is not a fit, I will tell you on the call and point you somewhere useful. Neither outcome costs you anything.

Principal
Ahmad Al Hinaiti

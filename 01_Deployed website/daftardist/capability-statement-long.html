01 · The practice

One practitioner.
Three practice areas.

Daftar Advisory is an independent finance practice serving founders, CFOs, and finance teams across MENA. The work is technical, the scope is fixed, the fee is named before the engagement begins, and the deliverable comes with the working file.

Eight years inside PwC and EY produced a clear observation. The middle of the MENA finance market is under-served. The Big Four are priced for large listed groups. Mid-tier firms are priced for compliance minima. Operators in between are over-billed when they buy the Big Four and under-supported when they don't. Daftar exists for that gap.

The Big Four sell you an audit file. Daftar sells the judgement that produced it.

05 · How it works

Four steps.
No ambiguity.

Step 01 · Scope

A 30-minute call to understand exactly what is needed.

If the fit isn't right, you'll hear that on the call — and a recommendation to a firm better positioned. Free. Same week.

Step 02 · Proposal

A one-page engagement letter with numbered scope.

Fixed fee, fixed timeline, payment schedule, and the non-attest disclaimer. You know what the work is and what it costs before it starts. Delivered within 48 hours of the scoping call.

Step 03 · Execute

The work is done directly.

Weekly checkpoints with drafts shared openly — not held until a grand reveal at the end. Typical engagement runs 1 to 6 weeks. Longer engagements break into milestones with explicit deliverables at each.

Step 04 · Deliver

The deliverable plus the working files.

Formulas, assumptions, sources. One review pass included; additional revisions priced per pass. Everything is yours to extend, share, or hand to your auditor.

Payment. Standard split is 50% on engagement letter signature, 50% on delivery. For engagements over USD 15,000 the split moves to 40 / 30 / 30 — letter, midpoint, delivery. Wire to a Jordan-based USD account; AED and SAR accepted on request.

06 · Where the refusal lives

When no is the right answer.

The practice wins by saying no to the wrong engagement. Five categories of work go elsewhere. A name and an introduction usually go with the referral.

01 · Attest engagements

Anything that requires a licensed auditor to sign.

Statutory audit, review, agreed-upon procedures. Daftar is non-attest advisory only — no JACPA, SOCPA, or UAE Ministry of Economy audit licence is held. If you need an attest signature, you need a licensed firm.

02 · Tax filings

The registered agent files.

ZATCA VAT and CIT returns, UAE FTA filings, JACPA tax returns. The practice supports the technical work and documentation that goes into the filing — but the registered tax agent files. Referrals available in all three jurisdictions.

03 · Engagements that need five people

Group audits, multi-entity TP studies, large ERP work.

If five practitioners are required, this is the wrong firm. Mid-tier and Big Four referrals available.

04 · Pre-decided conclusions

Independence is not for sale.

If the principal has already decided the answer and wants documentation that supports it, that is a job for someone else. Every engagement letter carries an independence clause. Recommendations are written on the evidence.

05 · Arabic-first regulatory relationship work

A practice in Riyadh or Jeddah is better placed.

Some engagements turn primarily on Arabic-first regulatory relationship management — extended ZATCA hearings, Zakat assessments under appeal, inheritance-trigger restructures. Two named referrals on request.
